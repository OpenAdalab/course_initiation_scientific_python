# Resources to go further

In this page you will find resources (course elements, chapters, online courses, videos, ...) that I have collected and assembled to facilitate your self-learning.

## Data Science

### Python programming

To find relevant resources according to your experience in python, please refer to **Chapter 2 of the Data Science course**.

### Object Oriented Programming in Python

If you want to learn more about object-oriented programming in Python, you can take these courses from Open Classrooms, depending on your needs:

- [easy level course](https://openclassrooms.com/fr/courses/4302126-decouvrez-la-programmation-orientee-objet-avec-python)
- medium level course](https://openclassrooms.com/fr/courses/7150616-apprenez-la-programmation-orientee-objet-avec-python)

### Small courses

Recently the kaggle platform has set up mini courses with corrected exercises by chapters that are quick to do. These courses will allow you to discover some fundamental notions, that you will have the opportunity to dig deeper afterwards.

I advise you, according to your level of knowledge, and by order of priority to follow the following mini courses:

- learning [pandas](https://www.kaggle.com/learn/pandas) the most used python module to manage tabular data
- introduction to [SQL basics](https://www.kaggle.com/learn/intro-to-sql) for database manipulation. Optionally, you can go further, exploring more advanced [SQL language features](https://www.kaggle.com/learn/advanced-sql), including learning how to combine multiple tables
- learn the [basic principles of data cleansing](https://www.kaggle.com/learn/data-cleaning) and can complete your knowledge by testing [machine learning methods for data pre-processing](https://scikit-learn.org/stable/modules/preprocessing.html#preprocessing-data) by exploring the dedicated menu of the sckit-learn framework. To go even further, you can test [semi-supervised learning algorithms for automatic data labeling](https://scikit-learn.org/stable/modules/label_propagation.html#semi-supervised) of the same framework
- finally you can finish your learning of the basics by studying [feature engineering](https://www.kaggle.com/learn/feature-engineering)

## Mathematics for data science and machine learning 

### Books

- In order to continuously deepen your learning of basic mathematics for data science and machine learning, feel free to browse through the chapters of the book [mathematics for machine learning](https://mml-book.github.io/) when you need to deepen some fundamental notions or to discover others.
- [An introduction to statistical learning](https://faculty.marshall.usc.edu/gareth-james/ISL/ISLR%20Seventh%20Printing.pdf) is a reference book, almost the bible of data science and machine learning!  
I advise you to consult it when you already have a basic level in data science and machine learning and you need to dive into the detailed mathematics of a particular concept

### Courses & videos

I invite you to continue learning throughout the course, as you need to go deeper into certain concepts.

- You will find on [this blog post about toward data science](https://towardsdatascience.com/mathematics-for-data-science-e53939ee8306) a very complete list of resources to acquire the fundamentals of math for data science

- I recommend you to read the [scikit-learn tutorial](https://scikit-learn.org/stable/tutorial/statistical_inference/supervised_learning.html?highlight=curse) page which introduces you to some basics of machine learning with examples that will allow you to learn how to program with this indispensable framework.

## Deep Learning

### General resources

The [tensorflow framework educational resources page](https://www.tensorflow.org/resources/learn-ml) offers a **very rich content** containing **books**, online courses, **mathematical concepts**, **tensorflow resources**, ... 
I strongly advise you to refer to it to develop your theoretical and technical knowledge (for machine learning and deep learning) and to help you to realize projects.

Although the French courses offered are of good quality, I advise you to follow the English courses, as they are generally more numerous and more complete.

### Books
[Deep learning](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10710-017-9314-z), by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio and Aaron Courville, is the reference  book on deep learning

### Elements of online courses
#### English-speaking

#### Free

- Hugo Larochelle's [neural networks discovery videos](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SGZ6BttHMPw&list=PL6Xpj9I5qXYEcOhn7TqghAJ6NAPrNmUBH) allow you to discover in 92 short presentations (rarely exceeding 15 minutes) key points to complete your understanding of certain topics

- The course offering the best compromise between completeness and time that I recommend you to watch first and in full (it lasts less than an hour), is this course [recent introduction to Deep Learning by MIT](http://introtodeeplearning.com/)
- This course [Standford](http://openclassroom.stanford.edu/MainFolder/CoursePage.php?course=DeepLearning) is one of the best and most complete on the subject. You can find the videos of the lectures [here](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL3FW7Lu3i5JvHM8ljYj-zLfQRF3EO8sYv)
- The [fast.ai](https://course.fast.ai/index.html) course is a very good course for programmers to learn the essentials of the technique and theory quickly. Focus especially on lesson 1.
- This [Michael Nielsen's course](http://neuralnetworksanddeeplearning.com/index.html) from his book, is very illustrated and will allow you to fine tune some particular points

### Paid
- This course by [Andrew Ng](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/deep-learning?edocomorp=mar19affiliate20off&ranEAID=vedj0cWlu2Y&ranMID=40328&ranSiteID=vedj0cWlu2Y-gVEh8s2dZJMcmeReNFALdA&siteID=vedj0cWlu2Y-gVEh8s2dZJMcmeReNFALdA&utm_campaign=vedj0cWlu2Y&utm_content=10&utm_medium=partners&utm_source=linkshare), one of the pioneers of deep learning, is available on the Coursera platform

## Tutorials & Exercises

### Deep Learning

- The [tutorial](https://www.tensorflow.org/tutorials) section of Tensorflow is complete and detailed. Start with the section for beginners. You will be able to run the code proposed in Colab, the notebook proposed by Google

- The [PyTorch tutorial](https://pytorch.org/tutorials/) section contains exercises and classic applications of deep learning. I particularly recommend the [*deep learning 60min blitz*](https://pytorch.org/tutorials/beginner/deep_learning_60min_blitz.html) to learn how to use the framework
